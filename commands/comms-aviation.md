# /comms-aviation [recipient] [goal] — Aviation-Voice Email/Message Drafter

Trigger: "write to [aviation contact]" / "draft an email to [FBO/A&P/pilot/school]"
Purpose: Draft outbound communications in ESSCO's voice for aviation-specific
recipients — with the right authority signals, industry credibility, and directness
that works in the aviation B2B world.

## ESSCO Voice (aviation context)
- Direct. Confident. No corporate fluff.
- Authority signal: "Since 1955" — use sparingly but don't hide it
- Peer-to-peer: write as one aviation professional to another
- Never oversell. Pilots and A&Ps smell BS from a mile out.
- Specificity wins: name the aircraft, the regulation, the part number
- Short is better. These people are busy and reading on their phone between flights.

## Recipient Profiles

**Pilot / Aircraft Owner**
- Motivation: keep flying safely, protect investment, pride of ownership
- Language: direct, technical but not condescending, peer tone
- Avoid: "amazing opportunity", "game-changing", corporate speak

**A&P / Avionics Shop (like Aero-Pro)**
- Motivation: accuracy, liability protection, staying current
- Language: regulatory context (FAR/AIM references), technical precision
- Avoid: vague promises, anything that sounds like you don't understand their world

**FBO / Flight School**
- Motivation: operational efficiency, student experience, cost control
- Language: volume/cost framing, operational benefit, proven reliability
- Avoid: consumer-facing language, "amazing deals"

**Distributor (Sporty's / Pilot Mall / Aircraft Spruce)**
- Motivation: margin, turnover, customer satisfaction
- Language: business terms, SKU/catalog framing, fulfillment reliability
- Avoid: retail language, emotional appeals

**Government / Military**
- Motivation: compliance, documentation, chain of accountability
- Language: formal, regulatory citations, institutional framing
- Avoid: casual tone, first names unless established relationship

**Industry Partner (JV / AirCorps Library type)**
- Motivation: mutual benefit, filling a gap, clean deal terms
- Language: straightforward business case, their gap + our capability
- Avoid: overselling, vague "synergy" language

## Steps

1. **Identify recipient and goal:**
   - Who (name + role + company if known)
   - What's the ONE thing you want them to do or know
   - Any existing relationship context
   Ask ONE question if missing critical info.

2. **Draft three versions:**

   **Direct** — shortest path to the ask. No throat-clearing.
   Subject: [specific, not clever]
   Body: [context in one sentence] + [the ask] + [why it's worth their time]
   Sign-off: Dale Berger / ESSCO Aircraft / dale@esscoaircraft.com / Since 1955

   **Warm** — same content, softer opening.
   Adds one sentence of genuine connection before the ask.

   **One-liner** — for text, Slack, or quick follow-up.
   The ask in one sentence. Nothing else.

3. **Flag anything Dale should verify before sending:**
   - Pricing or terms referenced
   - Regulatory claims
   - Commitments on timeline or capability

4. **Output format:**
```
TO: [name, role, company]
GOAL: [one sentence]

── DIRECT ──
Subject: [subject line]
[body]

── WARM ──
Subject: [subject line]
[body]

── ONE-LINER ──
[single sentence]

VERIFY BEFORE SENDING: [any flags — or "nothing flagged"]
```
